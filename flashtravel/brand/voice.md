# FlashTravel brand voice

## Who we sound like
A friend who books a lot of vacations and gets you a deal because she knows a guy. Warm, in-the-know, a little pushy when the clock is ticking — but never slick, never corporate.

## Who we don't sound like
- A luxury concierge ("the discerning traveler")
- A robotic deal aggregator ("save up to 47%")
- A cruise line's official voice ("embark on a journey")
- An AI assistant ("I'd be happy to help you find...")

## Words we like
family · vacation · getaway · deal · sail · trip · pack · ditch · escape · bring · book · grab · sand · pool · kids eat free · all-in · port · ship · island · resort

## Words we ban
luxury · luxurious · exclusive · elite · VIP only · premier · curated · bespoke · journey (as a noun) · embark · indulge · pamper · world-class · breathtaking · stunning · paradise (overused) · unwind (overused)

## Sentence shape
- Short. One thought per sentence.
- Start with a verb when you can. "Pack the kids."
- Drop articles for punch when it fits. "Sail Caribbean. 4 nights. Under $400."
- One exclamation point per piece of copy, max. None is fine.
- Ask a question if you can answer it in the next line.

## Subject lines
- 35–45 characters
- Specific number > vague claim. "$398 Bahamas" beats "Massive Caribbean savings"
- Time pressure when real, not when invented. "Ends Friday" only if it actually ends Friday.
- Lowercase is fine. Title Case is fine. ALL CAPS is not.
- No emojis in subject lines.

## SMS
- ≤160 characters total, including "Reply STOP to opt out"
- Lead with the offer. The link goes last.
- Sender name in the first 20 chars: "FlashTravel: ..."
- One sentence. Two if the second is the link + STOP line.

## Landing pages
- Hero answers three questions in 2 seconds: where, when, how much.
- Price is the biggest number on the page.
- One CTA. Repeat it twice on long pages, never different wording.
- No stock-photo "happy couple on a beach" — we use real supplier imagery.
- Trust strip near the fold: cruise line / resort logos, not customer testimonials in Phase 1.

## Ad copy
- Headline: 30 chars hard limit (Meta feed). 25 if you want it to look right on small screens.
- Primary text: 90 chars before the "see more" cutoff. Front-load the offer.
- One number, one place, one verb. "Sail to Cozumel — $349."

## Edge cases
- Kids: "kids" not "children." "Kids eat free" is a magic phrase, use it when true.
- Couples without kids: "you two," not "couples." Less clinical.
- Solo travelers: don't pity them. Just say "going solo? we've got single supplements waived on these dates."
- Seniors: don't say senior. Say "55+."

## When in doubt
Read it out loud. If it sounds like a TV ad for a cruise line, cut it. If it sounds like a text from your sister-in-law who works in travel, ship it.
